Why steel

Previously the fillament path in the hot-side of v9 was a tungsten carbide tube, this caused some problems.

Pressures within a hotend can be as high as 80 MPA (11.6 Kpsi), thus even the smallest gaps plastic will leak from. Typically the sealing surfaces of a hotend are elastically deformed during the tightening of the heatbreak and nozzle, but as you increase the hardness of a material, the force required to deform the surface increases. In the case of tungsten carbide the deformation force can be so high that the material would fracture before sealing. To get around this previously, the mating surfaces were lapped until they sealed on their own.

this was way too much work.

I am using a different strategy now, a method that still allows for the local elastic deformation of sealing surfaces but still is signifigantly harder than the fiberous fillers and in fact is as hard as tungsten carbide. It will remain a secret for now, but know that it is not CVD nonsense but a surface with real thickness.

Aditoinally with a hotend made of steel hot tightening is no longer required or advised for (x materials) nozzles.

The thermal conductivity of this grade of steel is approximately 50 W/mK. It is recommended to use MPC (model predictive control). To prevent discoloration limit the heater output to 80w during the initial heatup.

Watercooling

The hotend barbs are compatable with 3mm & 1/8" tubing. I recommend thick wall buna nitrile tubing.

I also procured some very nice quick connect fittings, You can have a set included for $30 (two plugs and two sockets). They make watercooling maintenance much less painful.
